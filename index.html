Common AI Patterns + Examples

The scanner checks for 13 mechanical patterns across three categories. No single pattern proves AI authorship. These signals are most meaningful in combination.

Language & Grammar
LanguageAI Vocabulary Density
LLMs overuse a predictable pool of elevated words — "pivotal", "delve", "tapestry", "meticulous", "nuanced" — that cluster together as a strong signal.
"This pivotal interplay is crucial to understanding the broader landscape."
LanguageCopulative Avoidance
LLMs replace plain "is/are" with elaborate constructions like "serves as", "stands as", or "represents". Studies show 10%+ fewer is/are in AI-edited writing.
"The gallery serves as the organization's primary space." (vs. "is")
LanguageNegative Parallelism — Not Just…But
AI frequently uses "not only X, but also Y" to appear balanced and nuanced — even when no clarification is needed.
"Not only does it reduce costs, but it also dramatically improves quality."
LanguageNegative Parallelism — Not X…But Y
AI negates one trait then affirms another — "It's not X, it's Y" — or uses inline comma-negation like "X, not the Y." Can span across two sentences.
"Infrastructure is the moat, not the model."
LanguageRule of Three
LLMs compulsively group items into exactly three — adjectives, nouns, or phrases — to make analysis feel comprehensive. High frequency is the signal.
"The platform is fast, reliable, and easy to use."
LanguageElegant Variation
LLMs swap synonyms to avoid repeating the same noun — "the entrepreneur", "the executive", "the business leader" — where a human would simply repeat it.
"Maria founded the company. The entrepreneur expanded. The executive later pivoted."
Style
StyleTitle Case Section Headings
AI capitalizes Every Main Word in headings. Most style guides call for sentence case — only the first word and proper nouns.
"## The Role of Technology in Modern Education" (AI signal)
StyleMechanical Bolding
AI bolds every instance of a chosen keyword rather than using bold sparingly for genuine emphasis. Flagged when bolded words exceed ~8% of total words.
"The primary objective is to optimize the workflow for maximum efficiency."
StyleEmoji in Structural Elements
AI chatbots decorate headings and bullet points with emoji. This is rare in formal human writing and a strong visual signal.
"🔑 Key Takeaway: The framework prioritizes speed over accuracy."
StyleEm Dash Overuse
LLMs use em dashes (—) in a "punched-up" style to emphasize clauses where a comma or colon would be more natural. Every em dash is flagged.
"The solution is elegant — simple in design yet powerful in execution."
StyleUnnecessary Tables
AI defaults to small markdown tables for data that reads more naturally as a sentence — typically 2–4 rows of simple key-value pairs.
A 3-row table showing "Founded | 2004 / HQ | Austin / Revenue | $2.1B" that could be one sentence.
Additional
AdditionalSycophantic Opener
AI trained with RLHF developed a reflex of opening with a compliment before answering — "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "Certainly!"
"Great question! Let's explore this topic together."
AdditionalFormulaic Conclusion
AI almost always ends by recapping what it just said. Human writers typically conclude with a new insight, not a summary of previous points.
"In summary, this approach offers a balanced combination of speed and reliability."
